The Code

Three rules. No exceptions. No negotiation. This isn't a suggestion box.

Rule 1: Salt Before You Suffer

Pre-load like you mean it.

Your body doesn't store sodium like it stores fat (unfortunately). Front-loading electrolytes before activity means your cells actually have what they need when things get intense. Waiting until you feel terrible is a rookie move. ur welcome

Field Manual

Three protocols. One mission. Stop being an under-salted disaster.

Pre-Flight

Before the chaos begins

Loading electrolytes before activity is like checking your parachute before jumping. Sure, you could skip it. Once.

The Protocol:

  • 30-45 minutes before go-time
  • One serving, full glass of water
  • No heroics, no doubling up
  • Just basic preparation like a functioning adult

Your cells will thank you by actually working. ur welcome

Loadout

During the grind

You're in it now. Your body is hemorrhaging minerals like it's trying to lighten the load for a sinking ship. This is not the time for rationing.

The Protocol:

  • Every 45-60 minutes of sustained effort
  • Sip, don't chug (your stomach will revolt)
  • Adjust based on intensity and conditions
  • If it's hot, you need more. If you're going hard, you need more. If both, you definitely need more.

Maintaining electrolyte balance during activity is the difference between finishing strong and becoming a cautionary tale.

Reset

After the damage is done

The work is done. You're depleted, tired, and possibly questioning your life choices. Now is when recovery either happens or doesn't.

The Protocol:

  • Within 30 minutes of finishing
  • Full serving with adequate water
  • Pair with actual food if you can manage it
  • Then rest, because you've earned it

Tomorrow's performance depends on today's recovery. Skip this step and you'll feel it. ur welcome

The Weird Advantage

Endurance.

While every other predator sprints and rests, sprints and rests, early humans just... kept going. Walking. Jogging. Following. For hours. Sometimes days. Until prey that could outrun us in any short race simply gave up and died of exhaustion.

The Cooling System

Here's the biological cheat code: humans are the greatest heat-dumping machines the animal kingdom has ever produced. We have more cooling glands per square inch than almost any other creature. We can regulate core temperature during sustained effort in ways that would kill most mammals.

This is why a human can run a marathon in the desert while a horse would die trying. This is why persistence hunting worked. This is why YOU exist.
